KareHero Funnel Architecture

4-stage cascade × 3 columns: Today, After MDB, and Missing. Shows exactly where the funnel leaks and what gets installed to fix it.

Funnel Cascade

Where attention becomes revenue — and where it does not.

KareHero's product works. The commercial process works. The two broken stages are at the top — awareness and outbound reach. These are the only stages that require external infrastructure.

Stage TODAY — What exists AFTER MDB — What gets built MISSING — What is absent
Top of Funnel
Attraction
🔴 Broken

Stephanie's professional network (Uber EMEA, Harvard, LocalGlobe VC). REBA events and Carers UK conferences. LinkedIn posts (3,483 followers). HR press coverage when it appears. All require buyers to already be engaged or already know KareHero exists.
✅ Channel live

28 HR Directors in active benefits review windows contacted by week two. Signal-based targeting: Employers for Carers members, Carer's Leave Act compliance reviewers, new Head of Benefits hires. 20 to 30 new targets added monthly as signals are found.
⚠️ Not yet built

Case study content for organic search (TSB, KPMG outcomes unpublished). CIPD authority positioning. Comparison page addressing "KareHero vs EAP" in buyer language. Backlink strategy to fix Google page one absence for "eldercare employee benefit UK."
Middle of Funnel
Alignment
🟡 Partial

When buyers engage, the product communicates well. Named FTSE 100 clients provide credibility. Care Sourcer acquisition adds scale credibility. The commercial team enters well-prepared. But buyers who find KareHero in a benefits review have already built an informal shortlist. KareHero enters late.
✅ Enters early

Outbound sequences position KareHero before the informal shortlist forms. The HR Director who received the lead list and the search gap email has KareHero framed as the product with deeper coverage before she opens Google. Lottie is no longer the default reference point.
⚠️ Nurture layer absent

No 20-week nurture sequence for buyers who engaged but are not yet in a window. No retargeting for buyers who opened content. Authority Engine not yet installed — the 95% who are not evaluating right now have no warm-up path.
Bottom of Funnel
Activation
🟢 Working

Matthew Johnson (CRO) is in place. The commercial process exists. Deals close with major employers — KPMG, Deloitte, BT Group, TSB. When KareHero gets into an evaluation, the product sells itself. Close rate and ACV are not the problem.
✅ Better context on entry

When the commercial team enters conversations sourced through MDB outbound, the HR Director has already seen the lead list, the competitive comparison, and the Carer's Leave Act framing. The first commercial call starts further down the funnel.
⚠️ Proof not deployed

TSB, KPMG, Deloitte outcomes not published as formal case studies. These proof points exist but are not being used in the commercial process at scale. A published TSB case study would close 30-second credibility questions before the first call.
Post-Sale
Compounding
🟡 Partial

Clients are successful (1M+ families supported, £10.6M care funding accessed). Employer outcomes are real. But outcomes are not documented, published, or converted into acquisition content. Each client is a self-contained relationship — not a case study that feeds the top of funnel.
✅ Flywheel starts

Case study production begins with TSB in month 2. Published outcome data becomes outbound email content ("A 5,000-person employer accessed £X in care funding for its employees in year one"). Each new client makes the next outbound campaign more specific and more credible.
⚠️ Referral system absent

No structured HR Director referral programme. KareHero's FTSE 100 clients have peer networks of hundreds of HR Directors — none of whom are being systematically introduced to KareHero by their peers through any structured programme.
Growth Feedback Loops

Three loops that compound over time.

Each loop self-reinforces. By month 6, the channel is stronger than it was in month one — without additional investment.

Employers for Carers Acquisition Loop

→Outbound reaches HR Director in active benefits review (EfC signal confirmed)
→KareHero enters evaluation with lead list and competitive comparison
→Product wins head-to-head (funding + legal vs Lottie's resource library)
→Employer signs. Employee outcomes documented.
→Outcome data becomes case study for next outbound campaign
→Each client shortens the next sales cycle ↩

Case Study Credibility Flywheel

→TSB, KPMG outcomes published as formal case studies
→Case studies submitted to REBA, People Management, Employee Benefits
→Editorial coverage generates backlinks and search presence
→KareHero appears on Google for "eldercare employee benefit UK"
→Inbound HR Director finds case study, recognises peer company
→Win rate increases. More case studies. Organic search grows. ↩

HR Network Intelligence Loop

→28 HR Directors contacted. Replies arrive (interest, timing, not fit)
→Every reply enriches targeting model (which signals produce replies)
→Signal model improves. Next campaign more precise.
→Reply rate increases month-on-month
→Clients refer KareHero into HR peer networks
→Channel compounds without additional spend ↩
Priority Action Summary

Fix these two things first.

🔴 Priority 1: Outbound channel (Top of Funnel)

No systematic way to reach HR Directors at the 230+ Employers for Carers members who have never been contacted. This is the primary revenue gap. MDB installs this in week one.

🔴 Priority 2: Case study publication (Post-Sale → Top of Funnel)

TSB, KPMG, and Deloitte outcomes exist but are not published. Each unpublished case study is a missed opportunity for search presence, credibility content, and peer referrals.

🟢 Working: Product and commercial process

KareHero's product wins every head-to-head evaluation. Matthew Johnson (CRO) closes deals at FTSE 100 scale. These do not need fixing — they need more at-bats.

🟢 Working: Named client credibility

KPMG, Deloitte, BT Group, TSB, British Airways on the client list gives KareHero more social proof than most pre-Series A companies. The proof exists. It needs to be deployed in outbound.
