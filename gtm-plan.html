The Five Pillars

How the KareHero channel is built.

Five acquisition pillars, each targeting a specific buying signal. Together they reach HR Directors in active windows through multiple entry points simultaneously.

1

Employers for Carers Network Mapping

The Employers for Carers network is the definitive signal list. 230+ member organisations covering 2.9 million UK employees. MDB maps each member company, identifies the Head of Benefits or Chief People Officer, and segments by buying signal strength (EfC pledge active, carer-friendly designation, or peer membership only).

Output: A tiered contact database of the 230+ EfC member companies with confirmed HR decision-makers. Updated quarterly as new members join.

ClayApolloLinkedIn Sales NavigatorEfC member directory
KPI: 100+ verified HR Director contacts from EfC network in 30 days
2

Carer's Leave Act Compliance Trigger

The Carer's Leave Act came into force April 2024. Thirteen months on, employers who did the statutory minimum are now in a second review wave: "Is one week's unpaid leave really our answer?" MDB tracks employers who made compliance announcements in 2024 and are now 12 to 18 months post-announcement — the natural review point.

Output: A list of employers who made public compliance statements in 2024 and have not yet publicly announced a benefit upgrade. These are the highest-priority warm targets.

Google AlertsHR press monitoringPeople ManagementEmployee Benefits
KPI: 40 to 60 compliance-triggered targets identified per quarter
3

Employee Assistance Programme Gap Channel

Large employers whose Employee Assistance Programme contract is up for renewal in the next 6 to 12 months are in an active benefits evaluation window. MDB identifies companies with procurement cycle signals — new Head of Benefits hire (typically triggers benefits review in first 90 to 180 days), job postings for benefits roles, or LinkedIn announcements of benefits platform changes.

Output: A rolling list of employers in benefits procurement windows, refreshed monthly from LinkedIn and HR press sources.

ClayLinkedInJob board signalsREBA
KPI: 20 to 30 Employee Assistance Programme-gap targets identified per month
4

FTSE 250 Anchor Client Leverage

KareHero's existing clients — KPMG, Deloitte, BT Group, Nationwide — are the strongest proof of concept for their HR Director peers. When these clients publish case studies (currently unpublished), outbound emails to their peer companies at similar-scale organisations will reference the specific employer: "Your peers at [Company] deployed KareHero and documented [outcome]. Here is how that translates to your workforce."

Output: Published case studies for TSB and KPMG as priority, with specific metrics. Outbound sequences using these case studies as proof lines for peer organisations.

HubSpotKareHero websiteREBA editorialEmployee Benefits
KPI: 2 published case studies within 90 days; peer referral conversions tracked
5

HR Director Content and Authority Engine

Stephanie's LinkedIn profile and the KareHero company page are the primary awareness channel for HR Directors who are not yet in a buying window. The Authority Engine (LinkedIn content flywheel + 20-week nurture) warms this audience over 18 months — so when the window opens, KareHero is already the provider they trust, not a cold vendor.

Three posts per week targeting specific HR buyer pain: hidden carer data, Carer's Leave Act benchmarking, NHS funding guidance for employees. Each post ends with a low-friction resource offer that creates a DM conversation.

12-Week Roadmap

What gets built, and when.

Three phases. Foundation, campaign, and authority. Each phase builds on the last.

Weeks 1 to 4 — Foundation

Infrastructure and first contacts

  • ICP confirmation with Stephanie and Matthew Johnson
  • 28-company list verified and loaded into HubSpot
  • Email sequences configured in HubSpot sequences
  • LinkedIn profile optimised for outbound activity
  • Tier 1 Trigger email deployed to 15 companies
  • First replies received and qualified
Weeks 5 to 8 — Campaign

Expand and optimise

  • Friction email deployed to Tier 1 non-replies
  • Tier 2 list (13 companies) added to campaign
  • LinkedIn sequence completed for all Tier 1
  • First output data: reply rate by signal type
  • 3 to 5 commercial conversations qualified
  • Monthly signal update: 20 to 30 new companies added
Weeks 9 to 12 — Authority

Content flywheel and case study

  • LinkedIn content flywheel live (3 posts/week)
  • 20-week nurture sequence activated for non-buyers
  • First case study drafted (TSB or KPMG)
  • Case study submitted to Employee Benefits, REBA
  • Search presence improvement as backlinks accumulate
  • First employer win documented and reported to investors
