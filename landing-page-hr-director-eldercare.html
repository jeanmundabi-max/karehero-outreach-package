The problem you cannot see

Your carers are already struggling.
They just have not told you yet.

Pain 01 — The Invisible Crisis
"I'm using all my lunch breaks for care calls. My manager has no idea. I'm terrified to tell them."

Hidden carers are your highest-risk employees. They carry the burden alone. Carers UK research confirms that 2 in 5 working carers never disclose their situation to their employer — not because they don't want help, but because they don't believe help exists. By the time they tell HR, the decision to leave is already made.

Pain 02 — The Retention Leak
"My mum had a fall last week. I spent three days on the phone trying to work out who was responsible for her next steps. I nearly didn't come back to work."

600 people a day leave the workforce to care (DWP, March 2025). Some of those are your senior managers. Your team leads. Your institutional knowledge. Their departure is not preceded by a resignation letter — it is preceded by months of quiet decline that your current benefit stack never detects.

The policy gap

The Act gave them leave.
It did not give them a solution.

Pain 03 — The Board Question
"The Carer's Leave Act passed. We gave them a week's leave. But that's not the problem — the problem is they come back and nothing has changed."

Most employers have tried a platform, an EAP, or a carer's policy. These are useful. They are not the same as specialist support. An employee who takes carer's leave returns to the same impossible situation — without care funding advice, without legal guidance, without someone who actually knows what adult social care looks like. They leave quietly.

Pain 04 — The Productivity Gap
"We know some of our people are struggling. We just can't see them."

Presenteeism — working while distracted by care responsibilities — costs UK employers £8.2 billion per year in lost productivity (Centrica modelling). The employee who is physically at their desk but mentally trying to coordinate a care placement, a hospital discharge, or a NHS Continuing Healthcare appeal is not performing. You will not see it on any dashboard until they are gone.

How KareHero works

Full adult care support.
From first question to final placement.

KareHero covers the complete care journey — the part that EAP, wellbeing platforms, and HR guides cannot. Every employee with a care need gets a dedicated adviser, not a chatbot or a signposting service.

01

Care Assessment

A qualified care adviser speaks with the employee to understand the care situation: type of care needed, urgency, existing support, and family circumstances. This is the conversation the employee could not have anywhere else.

Funding and Legal Navigation

KareHero advisers identify funding routes the employee did not know existed — NHS Continuing Healthcare, local authority funding, attendance allowance, Power of Attorney — and guide them through the process. This is where £10.6M in untapped funding has been unlocked to date.

Common questions

The objections HR Directors raise.
Honest answers.

We already have an EAP. Why do we need KareHero? +
EAP counsellors are trained in mental health support, not adult social care. When an employee's mother needs a care assessment, a hospital discharge appeal, or an NHS Continuing Healthcare application, an EAP counsellor will refer them elsewhere — back to square one. KareHero advisers are specialists in the adult care system. The EAP handles the emotional impact of caring; KareHero handles the practical navigation. Both have a role. They are not substitutes.
How many of our employees will actually use it? +
More than you expect. Research shows 1 in 5 of your employees is an active caregiver. Of those, 2 in 5 have never disclosed this to their employer. KareHero's proactive outreach model — not a passive resource employees must find — increases engagement significantly above EAP-level utilisation rates. More to the point: the cost of KareHero is exceeded by retaining a single senior employee who would otherwise have left. Utilisation is a secondary question.
We are not sure how many of our employees are carers. +
This is precisely the problem KareHero solves. Hidden carers are the highest-risk employees because they carry the burden alone — and because your organisation cannot support what it cannot see. KareHero's employer insights dashboard surfaces the patterns: where in your organisation carer stress is concentrated, what types of care situations are most common, and where the highest retention risk sits. You will know more about your caring workforce within 90 days than you have known in the last decade.
The board will ask about ROI. What do we tell them? +
The retention maths is straightforward: a 5,000-person employer faces approximately £3M in annual retention risk from carer attrition alone (1 in 5 carers, 10% burnout rate, £30,000 average replacement cost per employee). KareHero's annual cost represents a fraction of that exposure. At 10% retention improvement — 10 employees retained instead of leaving — the saving is £300,000. KareHero's cost is typically £40,000 to £150,000 for an employer of that size. The board question answers itself.
We are already a Carers UK member and have the Employer for Carers mark. Is KareHero different? +
The Employers for Carers network provides policy guidance, research, and community. It does not provide care advisers, funding navigation, or legal support for individual employees. KareHero installs the operational infrastructure that makes the designation meaningful — the evidence behind the commitment.
